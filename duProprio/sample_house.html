OPEN HOUSE THIS SUNDAY APRIL 28th BETWEEN 1PM AND 5PM: CONDO #1102 PHASE 2 AT THE NORDELEC (1740 ST-PATRICK STREET).

Superb condo (2015) with 2 bedrooms on the 11th floor (penultimate floor) in the prestigious and historic Nordelec offering a magnificent view of the vibrant neighbourhoods of Pointe-Saint-Charles, Little Burgundy and St-Henri. The huge industrial windows and south-west alignment guarantee the most beautiful sunsets in the city!

Some advantages of this unit:

  • Ultra bright, well insulated and therefore very economical ($30 per month only for energy costs!);
  • Open and well divided;
  • Extremely well soundproofed;
  • Many storage spaces including a walk-in in the master bedroom.

Some specifications on its location:

  • A 5-minute walk will bring you to the Lachine Canal, with its green spaces and beautiful bike path, and to the rue Notre-Dame with its trendy restaurants and bars!
  • The Atwater Market, Downtown and Old Montreal are 10 minutes by bike, 20 to 30 minutes walk. Ideal for work, outings and shopping! Charlevoix Metro is 1 km away. For your getaways to the South Shore, the Victoria Bridge is 3 minutes by car.
  • You'll soon be charmed by the neighbourhood life of Pointe-Saint-Charles, which has grown in popularity in recent years with its friendly pubs (The Knox, Microbrasserie 4 Origins, ...) and its nice local and international restaurants.

A few words about Nordelec:

The Nordelec is a heritage building (former manufacture of electric cables) of the early 20th century with a sober and elegant architecture.

All facilities are located within the building: cafés and services of all kinds (e.g. Caisse populaire Desjardins, chocolate factory, hairdresser...). In the areas for residents only you'll find:

  • a common room with kitchen, home theatre, fireplace and pool table;
  • a gym;
  • a rooftop terrace with barbecues and a swimming pool to enjoy the summer and the breathtaking panoramic view of Downtown, Mount-Royal and the South Shore!

Inclusions: oven, hob, extractor, fridge, washer/dryer and custom-made blinds! Possible inclusions (to be discussed): lights fixtures (Flos, Tom Dixon, Nemo) and other furniture (original Eames chairs, EQ3 marble table, imitation Wegner Shell Chair...).

Rapid moving in possible (otherwise very flexible for the moving in date).

A visit will charm you! Do not hesitate to call or email me if you have any questions. It will be a pleasure to answer any of your questions! You are also welcome to my OPEN HOUSE THIS SUNDAY APRIL 28TH BETWEEN 1PM AND 5PM!

How to buy this property

Found your dream home? Here’s a summary of steps to follow to become a homeowner. For detailed information, click here:

Buy on DuProprio.com
Did you know?

Whether you purchase a property from a homeowner or from a broker, the steps involved and the legal implications are the same. You’ll see that buying without an intermediary has many advantages!

Can I afford to buy this property?

Prior to visiting a property, you should verify whether you have the means to purchase it by meeting with a financial advisor. Don’t forget, you must have enough money to cover both a down payment and the expenses related to the transaction (notary, property transfer duties, etc.).

Is this property right for me?

Consult the listing to determine whether the property features meet your needs. If necessary, communicate with the homeowner to obtain additional information. You may then make an appointment with the homeowner for a visit and prepare a list of questions to ask him.

Do I need to conduct a property inspection?

Verifying the condition of the property prior to purchasing it is a good precaution to take. In order to do so, you can hire a home inspector. He will intervene at a later date. Be prepared to add the inspection conditions to the offer to purchase.

How much should I offer?

Easily familiarize yourself with the market by visiting DuProprio.com and by verifying the price that comparable properties in the area have sold for. You’ll get a better idea of how much you should offer. You can also hire a licensed appraiser.

How do I proceed with an offer to purchase?

Have a discussion with the homeowner in order to come to an agreement on the price, the conditions of the sale and the possession date. You can then complete the offer to purchase form.

Thanks to DuProprio, the seller benefits from a legal assistance service and can ask their legal questions in order to properly complete the document.

The sale will be sealed with a notary, but until then, nothing prevents you from consulting a notary to write-up an offer to purchase. Once the deed of sale is drafted, you will be the new homeowner!
